Nikola Tesla

Biography

Nikola Tesla, (born July 9/10, 1856, Smiljan, Austrian Empire [now in Croatia]â€”died January 7, 1943, New York, New York, U.S.), Serbian American inventor and engineer who discovered and patented the rotating magnetic field, the basis of most alternating-current machinery. He also developed the three-phase system of electric power transmission. He immigrated to the United States in 1884 and sold the patent rights to his system of alternating-current dynamos, transformers, and motors to George Westinghouse. In 1891 he invented the Tesla coil, an induction coil widely used in radio technology.
Tesla was from a family of Serbian origin. His father was an Orthodox priest; his mother was unschooled but highly intelligent. As he matured, he displayed remarkable imagination and creativity as well as a poetic touch.

Achievements

  • Alternating Current (AC) System: Tesla was a pioneer in developing the AC electrical system, which is now widely used for power generation and distribution.
  • Tesla Coil: The Tesla Coil is an electrical resonant transformer circuit used to produce high-voltage, low-current, high-frequency alternating-current electricity.
  • Wireless Communication: Tesla demonstrated the possibility of wireless communication, which later became the basis for modern radio communication.
  • X-rays: Tesla conducted research on X-rays and their potential uses, contributing to the development of X-ray technology.
  • Remote Control: Tesla invented a remote control mechanism, which he demonstrated in 1898 at Madison Square Garden, New York.
  • Induction Motor: Tesla invented the induction motor, which is now widely used in industry for powering machinery.
  • Tesla Turbine: Tesla invented the Tesla Turbine, a bladeless centripetal flow turbine which has potential applications in power generation and other industries.
  • Neon and Fluorescent Lights: Tesla invented the neon and fluorescent lights, which are now commonly used in lighting systems.
  • Earthquake Machine: Tesla designed a machine that he claimed could generate earthquakes, although there is no evidence that he ever built it.
  • Wireless Power Transfer: Tesla conducted research on wireless power transfer, which laid the groundwork for modern wireless charging technology.
